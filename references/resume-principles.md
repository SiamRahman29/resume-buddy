# Resume Principles

The shared knowledge base for Resume Buddy's skills. These are durable, transferable
resume-writing standards — distilled from the Dartmouth CPD Resume Guide and general
best practice. Skills cite this file instead of re-deriving the rules; update guidance
here once and every skill inherits it.

Read the section relevant to your task. Apply the principles honestly — **never
fabricate experience, titles, dates, or metrics to satisfy a rule.**

---

## 1. Writing bullets — the ATOP formula

Every experience bullet should follow **ATOP**: **A**ction verb + **T**ask/project +
**O**utcome/**P**urpose. Lead with a strong verb, state the concrete work, end on the
impact.

- Focus on **accomplishments and outcomes**, not a list of responsibilities. Describe
  context only as much as needed to make the achievement legible.
- **Quantify** wherever possible: %, $, counts, scale, time saved. A number turns a
  duty into an achievement.
- Use **active voice** and the right tense: present tense for current roles, past tense
  for prior ones.

**Before → After**
- *Before:* "Sell merchandise, stock shelves, and ensure store cleanliness."
- *After:* "Partnered with marketing to launch a new product-placement program;
  increased student traffic 35%."

**When a bullet has no number, do not invent one.** Flag it and tell the user exactly
which metric to find and how to compute it (e.g. "latency drop = (old − new) / old —
check the APM dashboard before/after"). Use a `[NUMBER NEEDED: …]` placeholder so the
gap is obvious.

## 2. Action-verb bank

Vary verbs; don't repeat the same opener. Never start a bullet with "Responsible for,"
"Helped with," "Duties included," or "Worked on."

- **Analytical:** Analyzed, Assessed, Appraised, Clarified, Compiled, Critiqued,
  Estimated, Evaluated, Extrapolated, Forecasted, Interpreted, Investigated, Processed,
  Reconciled, Resolved, Reviewed, Surveyed, Synthesized, Systematized, Validated
- **Communication / people:** Addressed, Authored, Collaborated, Composed, Convinced,
  Corresponded, Drafted, Edited, Elicited, Explained, Influenced, Lectured, Mediated,
  Moderated, Negotiated, Persuaded, Promoted, Publicized, Recruited, Translated, Wrote
- **Creative:** Adapted, Conceptualized, Created, Customized, Designed, Devised,
  Developed, Established, Fashioned, Founded, Initiated, Instituted, Integrated,
  Introduced, Invented, Originated, Planned, Revised, Revitalized, Shaped, Solved
- **Data / financial:** Administered, Allocated, Analyzed, Appraised, Audited, Balanced,
  Budgeted, Calculated, Computed, Estimated, Forecasted, Managed, Projected, Purchased,
  Quantified, Reconciled, Reduced, Researched, Tabulated, Tracked
- **Teaching:** Advised, Coached, Communicated, Demonstrated, Developed, Enabled,
  Encouraged, Evaluated, Facilitated, Fostered, Guided, Informed, Instructed, Taught,
  Trained, Tutored
- **Management / leadership:** Administered, Chaired, Consolidated, Coordinated,
  Delegated, Directed, Enhanced, Established, Executed, Improved, Increased, Initiated,
  Led, Managed, Orchestrated, Organized, Oversaw, Pioneered, Prioritized, Spearheaded,
  Supervised
- **Organizational:** Accomplished, Achieved, Arranged, Attained, Cultivated, Ensured,
  Expanded, Formalized, Generated, Implemented, Launched, Monitored, Overhauled,
  Redesigned, Secured, Streamlined, Surpassed, Targeted, Transformed, Upgraded
- **Research:** Analyzed, Collected, Compared, Conducted, Diagnosed, Examined,
  Extracted, Gathered, Identified, Interpreted, Interviewed, Investigated, Located,
  Summarized, Surveyed, Synthesized, Systematized, Tested

## 3. Do's & Don'ts checklist

Use this as a quality screen. (In **resume-import**, only *flag* violations — don't
rewrite; import faithfully reproduces the source.)

**Do**
- Be concise; lead with achievements and contributions.
- Quantify outcomes.
- List experiences in **reverse chronological order** (most recent first).
- Include GPA only if it's **3.0 or above**; include test scores only when requested.
- Include "Relevant Coursework" only when the courses are actually relevant to the role.
- Use a "Relevant Experience" section only if the experience is relevant to the target.
- Keep tense consistent (present for current, past for prior).
- Maintain **consistency** in font, sizing, dates, and punctuation throughout.
- Proofread for spelling and grammar; have a second person review.
- Submit as a **PDF** so formatting is preserved.

**Don't**
- Don't stop at description — always tie to an achievement or outcome.
- Don't use "I / me / my."
- Don't write "Responsible for" or "Duties included."
- Don't include an **Objective** statement at the top (use a targeted Summary instead).
- Don't add "References available upon request."
- Don't note whether positions were paid or unpaid.
- Don't include personal info, physical characteristics, or photos (rare arts/acting
  exceptions aside).
- Don't use insider jargon a general reader won't recognize.

## 4. Formatting standards

- **Margins:** 0.5–1.0 inch.
- **Body font:** a standard typeface (Helvetica/Arial/Times/Calibri equivalents),
  10–12 pt.
- **Name:** larger and bold, 14–18 pt, in a header with contact info.
- **Length:** one page for most candidates. Exceptions: academic CVs and public-sector
  resumes may run 2+ pages.
- **Consistency is the rule:** identical formatting for every date, heading, and bullet.
- For LaTeX masters, keep the preamble crisp: include `\usepackage[T1]{fontenc}` and a
  scalable-font package (`lmodern` or a `fontspec` face) so the PDF doesn't render as
  fuzzy Type 3 bitmaps.

## 5. Section structure

A resume has at least three to four prominent sections, in roughly this order:

1. **Name & contact information** (header: name, phone, email, LinkedIn/portfolio).
2. **Education** (school, degree/major/minor, graduation date, GPA if ≥3.0; honors,
   relevant coursework only if applicable).
3. **Experience** — the core. Can be split/renamed by purpose: *Relevant Experience*,
   *Research Experience*, *Leadership Experience*, *Additional Experience*, etc.
4. **Skills** (only if relevant — typically technical/computer and language skills;
   state honest proficiency levels).

A **targeted Summary** (3–4 sentences) may lead the resume — this is the modern
replacement for an Objective. See **resume-summarize**.

## 6. Resume types & ATS-friendliness

- **Chronological** — the default; reverse-chronological experience with outcome-focused
  bullets.
- **Targeted** — tuned to one role: mirror the posting's vocabulary and keywords, and
  reorder to surface the most relevant experience first. See **resume-tailor**.
- **ATS / plain-text ("e-resume")** — for online application portals. Avoid tables,
  images, text boxes, multi-column layouts, and keywords buried only in a header — these
  break automated parsers. Use standard section names and strong keywords from the
  posting. Keep a "pretty" version for humans and interviews.

---

## 7. Targeting a resume to a job description

- Read the posting for its **required skills, responsibilities, seniority, domain, and
  repeated keywords** — then mirror that language *where it's honest*.
- Reorder bullets and sections so the most JD-relevant experience comes first.
- Use the posting's exact terms for skills the user genuinely has (helps both the
  recruiter and the ATS) — never keyword-stuff with skills they don't have.
- Trim content that isn't relevant to the target if the resume runs long.

## 8. Industry-specific notes

- **CS / Engineering / STEM:** document technical projects and research; explain purpose
  and impact in plain terms; list specific skills/coursework when relevant and state
  honest proficiency.
- **Finance / Consulting:** quantify rigorously (deal size, %, $); emphasize analytical
  work, modeling, and measurable client/business outcomes.
- **Education:** two pages acceptable to show varied teaching exposure; a portfolio of
  teaching materials can supplement.
- **Public sector (government):** explicitly state how you meet each hiring criterion —
  reviewers (HR, not the hiring manager) won't infer anything. Can run 2–2.5 pages;
  include timeframes and outcomes; list everything, paid and unpaid.
- **Arts / entertainment:** more a listing of works/roles/exhibitions than a narrative
  of skills; acting resumes may include physical characteristics and a headshot.
- **International / global:** postings may request a "CV" but usually mean a 1–2 page
  resume; confirm with the contact and respect local hiring conventions.
